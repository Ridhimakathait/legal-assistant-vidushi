Frequently Asked Questions

You can file a complaint under the Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005. Contact your local police station or women's helpline for immediate assistance. You can also approach a lawyer to file a case for protection.

You can file an FIR at your local police station. You can go in person, send a written letter, or file online on the police website. Provide all relevant details and evidence. You have the right to obtain a copy of your FIR.

RTI is your right to request information from public authorities. You can file an RTI application with the government office. They must respond within 30 days. There's a small fee involved, typically ₹10.

Cyber harassment includes sending threatening messages, spreading rumors, or abusive content online. It's punishable under the IT Act. You can report it to the cyber cell and file a police complaint. Document all evidence.

Court cases typically take 2-5 years depending on complexity and court load. Civil cases may take longer than criminal cases. Regular follow-ups with your lawyer can help expedite the process.

Filing a Complaint - Step by Step

1

Gather Information

Collect all relevant details about the incident including dates, times, locations, and witness names.

2

Choose the Right Authority

Determine whether to file with police, cyber cell, labor department, or other relevant authority.

3

Prepare Your Statement

Write a clear, concise account of what happened. Be factual and include all relevant evidence.

4

File Your Complaint

Submit your complaint in person, by post, or online through the official portal.

5

Obtain Receipt

Get an acknowledgment receipt with a case/complaint number for future reference.

6

Follow Up

Regularly follow up on your complaint status and provide additional information if requested.

Women's Legal Rights

Protection from Harassment

Right to file complaints against sexual harassment at work, home, or in public places under POSH Act and IPC sections 354, 509.

Domestic Violence Protection

Protection under the Domestic Violence Act, 2005. You can seek orders for protection, residence, and maintenance from courts.

Equal Pay for Equal Work

Right to equal remuneration for same work or work of a similar nature as per the Equal Remuneration Act, 1976.

Maternity Benefits

Right to paid maternity leave, medical benefits, and protection from dismissal during pregnancy under the Maternity Benefit Act.

Property Rights

Full rights to own, inherit, and manage property. Married women have rights to husband's property as per succession laws.

Education and Employment

No discrimination in education or employment based on gender. Right to work in all professions and receive equal opportunities.
